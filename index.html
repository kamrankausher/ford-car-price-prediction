◆ Key Insights
WHAT DRIVES
THE PRICE

Factors our model uses to determine vehicle market value.

01
MILEAGE
Every mile tells a story. Low-mileage vehicles retain significantly higher resale value, with depreciation accelerating past 30,000 miles.
Strongest Negative Predictor
02
MODEL YEAR
Recent vehicles command premiums. Our data shows price increases of 8–12% per year for models manufactured between 2016–2020.
Top Positive Predictor
03
ENGINE SIZE
Larger engines reflect performance expectations. From the 1.0L EcoBoost to the 5.0L Mustang V8, displacement correlates strongly with price.
High Variance Factor
04
TRANSMISSION
Automatic and Semi-Auto gearboxes add a measurable premium over manual variants — typically £800–£2,000 depending on the model.
Categorical Predictor
05
FUEL TYPE
Hybrid and Electric vehicles attract significant premiums due to fuel savings and EV grants, while Diesel has softened post-2018 emission concerns.
Market Sentiment Factor
06
MPG RATING
Higher fuel efficiency signals modern engine technology. Vehicles with 50+ MPG often carry a running-cost premium factored into resale price.
Efficiency Multiplier

◆ How It Works
THE
PROCESS

From raw data to precise prediction in milliseconds.

01
INPUT DATA
Configure your Ford's specifications — model, year, mileage, engine, fuel type, transmission and MPG.
⚙️
02
ENCODE & SCALE
Categorical variables (model, fuel, transmission) are label-encoded. Numerical features are StandardScaler normalised.
🔢
03
FOREST VOTE
200 decision trees each make an independent prediction. The ensemble average eliminates overfitting and reduces variance.
🌲
04
VALUATION
The predicted price is returned with a ±8% confidence band representing realistic market variance for your configuration.
